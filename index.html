Visit Sweden

Shopping Malmö


Malmö makes for eclectic shopping. It’s got a 16th century shopping market square, a beloved flea market, a catalogue of shopping centres and new up-and-coming shopping areas.

Direct flights to Åre


Åre in Sweden is Northern Europe's largest and most advanced and diverse alpine sport resort. And it's not just the skiing that keeps people coming here, this place is well known for it's sizzling nightlife. From December 2016 you can fly direct to Åre Östersund airport from London Gatwick.

A Traditional Swedish Christmas

Christmas in Sweden sees its cities, towns and villages glow against the white winter landscape. It’s Christmas card perfect. Deep in the heart of Dalarna, central Sweden, you can also meet Santa’s reindeer, visit Santa’s workshop and see the little Christmas present storage.

Swedish music phenomenon

If you haven’t heard ‘Dancing queen’ by ABBA, or ‘One’ by dance collective Swedish House Mafia, you must have been living under a rock. Because music from Swedish musical artists is massive everywhere.

Meet the Original Hipsters

A thousand years before the first sourdough was set to ferment in the trendy neighbourhoods of Stockholm, crispbread bakers walked from farm to farm and baked up the rye harvests. We went looking for the origins of the modern hipster in the Swedish countryside.

Crime Fiction - West Sweden

Murder and mystery are in the air on the west coast of Sweden, at least in the books of Swedish crime writers Camilla Läckberg, Åke Edwardsson and Ann Rosman.

Sweden’s congestion taxes

Information from the Swedish Transport Agency: In Sweden both Swedish and foreign-registered vehicles pay congestion taxes in Stockholm and Gothenburg and bridge tolls in Motala and Sundsvall.

World famous Swedish glass

The Kingdom of Crystal (Glasriket) is where world famous Swedish glass such as Kosta Boda and Orrefors are made. Here you can visit glassworks and glass studios, blow your own glass and stay in a hotel featuring a glass bar and oodles of glass artwork.

Swedish culture

The clothes you wear, the online music you listen to, the books you read, the movies you watch, the furniture you buy. Chances are that there is a little bit of Sweden in there somewhere.

Food & Drink

The interest in Swedish cuisine is still going strong and this year two Swedish restaurants were included in the World´s 50 best, with Frantzen now at 31st place, and Magnus Nilsson´s restaurant Fäviken, located in the region of Jämtland, which came in at number 25. Esperanto is in the top 100 best restaurants at number 97.
